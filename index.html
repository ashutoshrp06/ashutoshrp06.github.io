About Me

The person behind the code

So here is the deal. I spent over three years at Juniper Networks wrestling with C++ codebases that were older than some of my interns. Telemetry systems, OpenConfig implementations, the kind of stuff that makes or breaks network infrastructure. I got pretty good at finding bugs that others missed. Also at drinking questionable amounts of coffee.

Now I am at The University of Edinburgh, pursuing an MSc in Artificial Intelligence. The goal? Bridge that awkward gap between brilliant research models and systems that can actually survive in the wild. Production ML is a different beast entirely, and someone needs to tame it.

When I am not debugging or studying, you will find me watching Arsenal lose the titles in creative ways, tracking Lewis Hamilton's every move, or playing cricket badly but enthusiastically. I believe good code is like good comedy. Timing is everything, and nobody laughs at your clever solution if it crashes in production.

A production-grade CLI tool for network engineers who debug infrastructure, not dashboards. Automating Benchmark Construction in Finance

LLM benchmarks have a shelf life shorter than milk. They saturate, get contaminated, and rarely fit your actual use case. Building new ones is expensive. My dissertation explores automated dynamic benchmarking using multi-agent LLM systems to generate meaningful evaluation data on the fly. Distributed Sparse Matrix Engine

A high-performance distributed sparse matrix multiplication engine built on Apache Spark and Scala. Because apparently multiplying matrices the normal way was too easy.
